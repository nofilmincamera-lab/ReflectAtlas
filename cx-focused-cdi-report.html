CX Composite CDI: 78.4 (Critical Drag)
Weighted composite (RCL 35%, PTM 45%, TLD 20%) places NexTel in the 91st percentile for CX drag. The 82 PTM score is exceptionally high, indicating severe process-technology disconnect. CX industry median: 59.2. Telecommunications sector median: 61.8.

CX-Specific Drag Drivers

Agent Cognitive Overload

CSR roles span 8-12 distinct Detailed Work Activities across billing, technical, account, and sales functions. Agents switch between 4-6 systems per interaction. Knowledge retrieval is manual search across multiple databases. This breadth drives 81 RCL score.

Manual Process Persistence

Despite having contact center platform, CRM, and knowledge base, actual workflows remain manual: agents type summaries, search knowledge manually, copy-paste between systems, and follow paper checklists. Technology exists but isn't integrated into workflow. 82 PTM score reflects this severe disconnect.

Workforce Composition Imbalance

62% generalist CSR concentration creates high-volume, low-leverage model. Limited technical specialists (12% vs. 18% benchmark) means more escalations and lower first-call resolution. Underweight automation enablers means no internal capacity to build/maintain intelligent tools. TLD of 72 reflects this imbalance.
